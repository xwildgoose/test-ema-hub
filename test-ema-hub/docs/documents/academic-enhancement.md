# Academic Enhancement Programmes (CeLEARN)

Through Continuing Education and Lifelong Learning (CeLEARN), UB offers academic
enhancement/enrichment programmes for people who want to pursue an associate or
bachelor degree at UB but don't yet meet the academic qualifications for direct entry.

## Academic Enhancement (ages 16–24)
Recent high school graduates aged 16 to 24 who don't meet direct-entry requirements
may enrol in UB's Academic Enhancement Programme — a seven-week academically
intensive enrichment experience. Students take English Language, Mathematics, Biology,
Chemistry, Geography, General Science and History, and must complete each with a
minimum grade of "C" within the seven weeks. Some courses are available web-based
through the Open Learning Department.

## Academic Enhancement — Mature Students (ages 25+)
For adults 25 and older who don't meet direct-entry requirements but wish to enrol in a
UB associate or bachelor degree. Offered in fall (August), spring (January) and summer
(May). Students take non-credit courses in Mathematics and English.

- Students interested in Nursing, Biology and/or Chemistry must complete Academic
  Enhancement Biology (BIOL 071) and Chemistry (CHEM 071) if they don't have GCE or
  BGCSE passes at grade "C" or higher in those subjects.
- Students pursuing a degree in primary education must complete Academic Enhancement
  General Science (SCIN 070).

## How to Apply
1. Complete the CeLEARN Application for Admission — Academic Enhancement Programmes.
2. Pay a non-refundable processing fee of $40.00 (cash, bank certified cheque, debit
   card, or credit card — Visa, MasterCard, Suncard) at the Business Office,
   9:00 a.m.–4:00 p.m. weekdays.
3. Submit the completed application form, proof of payment, and supporting documents
   (copy of relevant passport pages showing passport number, photo ID and expiry;
   self-statement; and credentials including any BGCSEs, BJCs or equivalents) to the
   Office of CeLEARN, 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. weekdays.
4. Request an official transcript be mailed directly from your last school to CeLEARN —
   P.O. Box N-4912, Nassau, The Bahamas, or P.O. Box F-42766, East Grand Bahama
   (for applicants studying at UB North).

## Acceptance
Applicants are notified in writing once documents are processed. Successful applicants
receive a Letter of Acceptance stating their UB student ID number, the programme and
campus they've been accepted into, and their date for new student orientation and
registration.

## Accepting an Offer of Admission
To accept, students must:

- Notify the Office of CeLEARN;
- Attend new student orientation;
- Activate and use their UB email account;
- Participate in advisement and register during the designated period;
- Pay all tuition and fees;
- Collect their UB student ID card from the Office of Recruitment and Admissions;
- Submit the completed Medical Record form to the Campus Nurse by the last Friday in
  March (fall admits) or the last Friday in October (spring admits). Failure to submit
  by the due date results in a medical hold that blocks registration until cleared.
  All students must provide documentary evidence of MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)
  vaccination;
- Submit the completed Accident Insurance Waiver Form and proof of accident insurance
  to the Campus Nurse by the last Friday in August (fall admits) or the last Friday in
  October (spring admits) if they don't want automatic coverage under the Student
  Accident Insurance Protection Plan.

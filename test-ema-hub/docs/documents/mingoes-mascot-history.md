# UB Mingoes — Mascot & History

**Blu the Flamingo** is the mascot for UB Athletics (the Mingoes). Blu is an
anthropomorphic blue flamingo wearing the Mingoes basketball jersey, number 0.

## Origins
Earlier College of The Bahamas (COB) athletic names included the Flamingoes and later
the Caribs. The move from College of The Bahamas to University of The Bahamas marked the
institution's first time creating its own mascot.

The flamingo was chosen as the Mingoes' official mascot in 2016 by Athletic Director
Kimberley Rolle and then-President Rodney Smith, Ph.D., alongside conceptual designer
Renbert Mortimer II, after a university search committee called for submissions (with a
$5,000 campus competition and outreach to Bahamian marketing companies). A condition was
set that the mascot must be blue.

RGMII Waynehead Productions pitched three ideas — "U B Rare Breeds" (flamingo-based),
"U B Luscas" (half shark, half octopus), and "U B Chickcharney." After consultation with
staff, faculty, students, student-athletes and the UB Alumni Association plus market
research, "U B Mingoes" became the official brand name, derived from "U B Rare Breeds."
A flamingo-based mascot aligned with the flamingo on UB's official shield while avoiding
brand confusion.

## Naming the Mascot
The mascot was originally named "Fury" by its designer. A naming campaign began in 2018
with finalists including Bey/Bei/Bui, Mingoe, Blu, Fly/Flight, Fury, and Blue Fury.
**Blu** won the majority vote.

## The "U B, Mingoes" Chant
The chant was pitched at the brand's initial meeting and made famous by Traven Cargill
and the UB Athletics Department, who first shared it on Charter Day. The "fla" was dropped
from "flamingoes" so "Mingoes" matches the syllables of "U B," and the echoing "U B"
mimics the honking and echoes of a flock of flamingoes.
